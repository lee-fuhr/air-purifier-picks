Frequently asked questions

Can a cheap air purifier actually work?

Yes for small rooms. The Levoit Core 300 uses genuine True HEPA and has a credible CADR for rooms under 200 sq ft. The sacrifice at low prices is room coverage area and features (no air quality sensor, no smart controls), not fundamental filtration quality.

What do you give up with a budget air purifier?

Typically: larger room coverage, air quality sensors, auto mode, smart home integration, and app control. The core filtration, True HEPA capturing allergens and particles, is achievable at budget prices. The intelligence that adjusts the purifier to your air quality is what costs more.

Is the Levoit Core 300 worth buying?

Yes, for rooms under 200 sq ft. It has True HEPA, multiple filter options (standard, toxin absorber, pet allergy, mold and bacteria), and an almost-silent sleep mode. For a bedroom or home office, it's the best small-room value available.

Are budget air purifier filters cheap too?

Replacement filters on budget purifiers typically cost $15-25. The lower purchase price often offsets with similar or slightly higher filter cost percentage. At $60-100 for the unit, a $20 annual filter is 20-30% of purchase price, similar ratio to premium units.

Should I spend more for a better purifier?

The Coway AP-1512HH at ~$120 is the sweet spot for bedroom-sized rooms, the CADR-to-price ratio is excellent and the air quality indicator adds meaningful utility. For under $100, the Levoit Core 300 covers small rooms well. Below $50, filtration quality becomes unreliable.
