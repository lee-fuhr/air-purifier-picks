Coway vs Levoit air purifiers

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

Coway and Levoit both make excellent air purifiers, but they serve slightly different needs. Coway models generally offer higher CADR ratings and longer-lasting filters, making them a better choice for larger rooms or people with strong allergy needs. Levoit is more budget-friendly, with quieter operation and smart features on some models, ideal for smaller spaces and cost-conscious buyers.

How do CADR ratings compare between Coway and Levoit?

Clean Air Delivery Rate (CADR) is the most objective way to gauge how quickly a purifier cleans a room. Coway’s popular models, like those in the Airmega series, typically post higher CADR numbers for both smoke and dust, which means they can handle larger spaces, often up to 300+ square feet, more efficiently. Levoit’s lineup, especially the Core series, is designed for smaller rooms. Their CADR ratings are lower, but still effective for bedrooms, home offices, or apartments. If you’re placing a unit in a big open-plan living area, Coway’s higher CADR will deliver cleaner air faster. In a compact room, Levoit’s numbers are more than sufficient.

Which brand has cheaper filter replacements?

This is where the two brands diverge significantly. Coway filters are more expensive upfront, but they also last longer, many models use a permanent pre-filter you only need to wash, plus a true HEPA filter that may last a year or more before replacement. Over two or three years, the annual cost is moderate but not cheap. Levoit’s replacement filters are notably budget-friendly, which appeals to cost-conscious owners. The trade-off is that they need to be swapped more frequently, often every six to eight months depending on usage. If you prefer a “set it and forget it” approach, Coway’s longer intervals might save you hassle. If keeping ongoing costs low matters most, Levoit’s cheaper replacements give it the edge.

Do Coway or Levoit purifiers have smart features?

Levoit has embraced smart-home integration more aggressively. Several of its mid-range and premium models come with Wi-Fi, companion apps that let you monitor air quality, schedule run times, and even use voice assistants like Alexa or Google Assistant. That kind of convenience appeals to tech-savvy users who want control from their phone. Coway, by contrast, keeps things simpler. Most of its purifiers include basic auto mode, where the fan adjusts speed based on a built-in particle sensor, but you won’t find an app or Wi-Fi in their standard lineup. If you value simplicity and don’t need remote monitoring, Coway’s straightforward approach works fine. If you want to check air quality from your couch or program the unit while away, Levoit is the better bet.

Is one quieter than the other?

Noise levels vary by model and fan speed, but Levoit generally earns praise for being whisper-quiet on low settings, often around the level of a gentle fan in a library. That makes it a favorite for bedrooms where you want air cleaning without sleep disruption. Coway models are still quiet on lower speeds, but they can become noticeably louder on higher speeds, especially in larger units pushing more air. That said, both brands offer dedicated sleep modes that dim lights and drop fan speed to near-silence. For daytime use in a living room, the difference is minor; for nighttime use, Levoit has a slight advantage in perceived quietness.

Which brand fits different price tiers?

Levoit covers the entry-level and mid-range market exceptionally well. Its smallest purifiers are very affordable, and even its step-up models with smart features remain a few hundred dollars at most. For someone on a tight budget or looking for a secondary unit for a small room, Levoit is hard to beat. Coway occupies a higher price tier overall. Its units cost more upfront, but they are built with long-lasting filters and robust builds that many owners report lasting for years. You get premium materials, excellent CADR per dollar, and often a more comprehensive warranty. If your budget allows and you want a purifier that can handle a large living area, the extra spend on a Coway model often pays off in performance and durability.

Which brand is better for allergies or large rooms?

For serious allergy or asthma concerns, especially if you have pets or live in a polluted area, Coway’s higher CADR and dense HEPA filtration make it a strong first pick. Their larger units can turnover the air in a big room several times per hour, which matters more for reducing triggers like pollen, dust mites, and pet dander. Levoit still uses true HEPA filters and does a fine job in medium rooms. If your space is under 200 square feet and your allergies are moderate, a Levoit will likely suffice and cost less to run. But if you need to cover a great room or an open floor plan, or if you want the highest possible particle removal, Coway is the more capable choice.

Frequently asked questions

Which Coway or Levoit model is best for allergies?

For strong allergy relief, look at Coway’s mid-size Airmega models with a high CADR and a washable pre-filter. For smaller rooms, Levoit’s Core series with a true HEPA filter is still very effective against pollen and dust.

Does Levoit use a true HEPA filter?

Yes, most Levoit air purifiers use a true HEPA filter that captures 99.97 percent of particles as small as 0.3 microns. Just check the specific model, as some very low-end variants may use a HEPA-type filter instead.

Are Coway filters washable?

The pre-filter on Coway purifiers is washable, which helps extend the life of the main HEPA filter. The HEPA filter itself is not washable and needs to be replaced according to the manufacturer’s schedule.

Which brand uses less electricity?

Both brands are Energy Star certified on most models, so they are efficient. Levoit’s smaller units tend to draw slightly less wattage, but the difference in annual electricity cost is negligible for typical use.

Can I control Levoit with a voice assistant?

Yes, several Levoit models with Wi-Fi support Alexa and Google Assistant voice commands. You can turn the unit on or off, adjust fan speed, and check air quality hands-free.

Which brand offers a better warranty?

Coway typically includes a longer warranty on its purifiers, often several years on the unit and motor. Levoit’s warranty is shorter but still covers a few years; both companies have responsive customer support.

In shortUltimately, both brands deliver clean air, but your choice hinges on room size, budget, and whether you want app control. Coway excels in raw filtration power and filter longevity, while Levoit offers a more wallet-friendly entry point with modern connectivity. Consider your space and priorities, and you’ll find a match in either lineup.
