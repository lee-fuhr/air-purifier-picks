How we choose our picks

We don’t run our own lab tests. Instead, we comb through hundreds of long-term owner reviews, read teardowns from independent engineering blogs, and consult published expert reviews from reputable outlets (Wirecutter, Consumer Reports, and CNET). This gives us a clear picture of which models hold up after six months, a year, or more, and which ones develop rattles, sensor drift, or motor noise. We also pay close attention to what actually breaks: fans that seize up, filters that are hard to find or overpriced, and plastic housings that warp. By cross-referencing real complaints with the consensus from seasoned reviewers, we can confidently recommend machines that are built to last and perform well in everyday homes, not just in a controlled lab.
